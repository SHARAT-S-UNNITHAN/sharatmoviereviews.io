Retro - Movie Review

Movie Title: Retro

Genre: Romance, Comedy, Action

Director: Karthik Subbaraj

Release Date: 2025

Cast: Suriya, Pooja Hegde, and supporting actors in pivotal roles

Review

Retro, directed by Karthik Subbaraj, is not just a film—it’s a grand emotional journey. With its subtitle "Love-Laughter-War," the movie dares to tell a story that spans the spectrum of human emotions, delivering a cinematic experience that lingers in your heart long after the credits roll. Starring Suriya and Pooja Hegde in roles that feel tailor-made for them, Retro is an extraordinary blend of romance, humor, and action, all wrapped in a visually stunning package.

Plot & Storyline

Set in the vibrant 1970s, Retro transports us to an era of timeless love and volatile conflicts. The narrative centers on Arjun (Suriya), a charming and rebellious man whose free-spirited nature hides a deep commitment to justice and love. His life takes a drastic turn when he crosses paths with Meera (Pooja Hegde), a headstrong and compassionate journalist with a penchant for uncovering the truth. Their romance blossoms in unexpected circumstances, filled with lighthearted moments of laughter and heartfelt exchanges. However, their idyllic world is shattered by a ruthless warlord seeking control over their small coastal village. The story morphs into a gripping tale of love and survival as Arjun and Meera navigate betrayals, sacrifices, and battles to protect their people.

Performances

Suriya is a revelation as Arjun, capturing the essence of a man torn between love and duty. Whether he’s romancing Meera with playful charm, delivering powerful monologues, or executing intense action sequences, Suriya is at his peak, offering one of his most memorable performances to date. Pooja Hegde, as Meera, matches Suriya’s energy with a portrayal that is both fierce and tender. Her role as a journalist committed to uncovering the truth adds depth to the film. The chemistry between Suriya and Pooja is electric, making their relationship the emotional core of the film. The supporting cast, including veteran actors in pivotal roles, enriches the narrative with authenticity and complexity.

Direction & Screenplay

Karthik Subbaraj’s mastery lies in his ability to weave multiple genres seamlessly. In Retro, he blends romance, humor, and action without letting any aspect overpower the other. The screenplay is impeccably paced, ensuring every scene contributes to the narrative’s emotional and thematic depth. Subbaraj’s signature style of blending social commentary with mainstream appeal is evident, and his decision to set the story in the 1970s adds a vibrant aesthetic and a politically charged backdrop that enhances the drama.

Cinematography & Music

The visual storytelling in Retro is nothing short of spectacular. Shreyaas Krishna’s cinematography captures the rustic charm of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, the misty romance of Ooty, and the bustling chaos of Chennai with equal finesse. The period details are meticulously crafted, immersing the audience in the 1970s era. The music, composed by Santhosh Narayanan, is an inseparable part of the experience, with soulful melodies heightening the romance and pulsating background scores intensifying the action.

Pacing & Editing

The film’s pacing is flawless, balancing quieter emotional moments with high-energy action sequences. The editing ensures a cohesive flow, maintaining engagement throughout the runtime. Subbaraj’s ability to keep the love story and drama equally compelling is a testament to the film’s craftsmanship, with action sequences—from hand-to-hand combat to large-scale battles—woven seamlessly into the narrative.

User's Personal Opinion

Retro stands out for its ability to weave universal themes of love, resilience, and justice into a mainstream narrative. The exploration of social justice, journalistic integrity, and self-sacrifice through a 1970s lens feels both nostalgic and relevant. The film’s balance of intimate personal moments and epic collective struggles makes it a captivating experience for diverse audiences.

Final Thoughts

Retro is a cinematic triumph that showcases the best of Indian filmmaking. With its gripping narrative, stunning visuals, and unforgettable performances, it’s a testament to Karthik Subbaraj’s genius and the boundless talent of its cast and crew. Whether you’re a fan of romance, action, or simply great storytelling, Retro is a must-watch—a love letter to cinema itself.

Rating: 5/5

Final Thoughts: Retro is an experience that resonates long after the credits roll, a masterpiece for the ages.
