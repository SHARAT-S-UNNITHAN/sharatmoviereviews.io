Shaitaan - Movie Review

Movie Title: Shaitaan

Genre: Supernatural Horror, Thriller

Director: Vikas Bahl

Release Date: 8 March 2024

Cast: Ajay Devgn, R. Madhavan, Jyothika, Janki Bodiwala, Anngad Raaj

Review

Shaitaan, directed by Vikas Bahl, is a gripping supernatural horror thriller that explores the dark realms of black magic and the resilience of familial bonds. A remake of the 2023 Gujarati film Vash, this Hindi adaptation captivates with its unsettling atmosphere and powerful performances, particularly from R. Madhavan and Janki Bodiwala, though it stumbles in its predictable climax.

Plot & Storyline

Shaitaan follows Kabir Rishi (Ajay Devgn), his wife Jyoti (Jyothika), and their children, Janhvi (Janki Bodiwala) and Dhruv (Anngad Raaj), as they head to their remote farmhouse for a weekend getaway. Their peace is shattered when they encounter Vanraj Kashyap (R. Madhavan), a seemingly friendly stranger who uses black magic to hypnotize Janhvi, forcing her to obey his sinister commands. As Vanraj’s motives unfold—revealing a plan to sacrifice Janhvi in a dark ritual—Kabir and Jyoti fight desperately to save their daughter, leading to a tense battle of wits and willpower.

Performances

R. Madhavan steals the show as Vanraj, delivering a chilling performance that oscillates between charm and menace. His magnetic presence makes the antagonist truly terrifying. Janki Bodiwala, reprising her role from Vash, is exceptional as Janhvi, conveying the horror of possession with raw intensity. Jyothika shines as the fierce, protective mother, while Ajay Devgn’s portrayal of Kabir, though solid, feels restrained until the climax. Anngad Raaj adds emotional depth as the vulnerable Dhruv.

Direction & Screenplay

Vikas Bahl crafts a tense and atmospheric thriller, effectively building suspense without relying on CGI or jump scares. The screenplay by Aamil Keeyan Khan keeps viewers engaged, particularly in the first half, with its focus on psychological horror. However, the second half falters with predictable tropes and unresolved plot points, such as Vanraj’s backstory, which weakens the narrative’s impact.

Cinematography & Music

Sudhakar Reddy Yakkanti’s cinematography creates an eerie ambiance, with the isolated farmhouse setting enhancing the sense of dread. The tight framing in intense scenes, like the swing sequence, amplifies tension. Amit Trivedi’s background score is a standout, adding depth to the film’s unsettling tone, though the songs, including “Khushiyaan Bator Lo” and “Aisa Main Shaitaan,” feel unnecessary and disrupt the pacing.

Pacing & Editing

The film’s 132-minute runtime is engaging for the most part, with a strong first half that builds suspense. However, the second half drags slightly due to repetitive sequences and a cliched climax. Sandeep Francis’s editing is smooth but could have been tighter in the latter half to maintain momentum.

User's Personal Opinion

Shaitaan excels as a psychological thriller that taps into primal fears of losing control and protecting loved ones. Its exploration of black magic is intriguing, and the performances elevate the film beyond its narrative flaws. While the predictable ending and lack of depth in Vanraj’s motives are drawbacks, the film’s unsettling atmosphere and strong cast make it a compelling watch for horror enthusiasts.

Final Thoughts

Shaitaan is a solid addition to Bollywood’s sparse supernatural horror genre, driven by R. Madhavan’s menacing performance and Janki Bodiwala’s haunting portrayal. Despite a formulaic climax, its atmospheric tension and emotional core make it a worthwhile experience. Fans of psychological thrillers will find plenty to appreciate in this tale of good versus evil.

Rating: 3.5/5

Final Thoughts: Shaitaan delivers chills and thrills with standout performances, but its potential as a genre-defining horror film is hindered by a safe, predictable resolution.
