Uri: The Surgical Strike - Movie Review

Movie Title: Uri: The Surgical Strike

Genre: War, Action, Drama

Director: Aditya Dhar

Release Date: 11 January 2019

Cast: Vicky Kaushal, Paresh Rawal, Yami Gautam, Mohit Raina

Review

Uri: The Surgical Strike, directed by Aditya Dhar, is a gripping and patriotic war drama that captures the 2016 surgical strikes carried out by India on terror camps in Pakistan-occupied Kashmir. The film, with its powerful narrative and intense performances, showcases the bravery, sacrifice, and emotional turmoil faced by the Indian Army during this covert military operation.

Plot & Storyline

Uri: The Surgical Strike follows the story of Major Vihaan Singh Shergill, portrayed by Vicky Kaushal, who is deeply affected by the brutal killing of Indian soldiers in a terrorist attack in Uri, Kashmir. The film traces Major Vihaan’s emotional journey as he participates in the secretive military operation to avenge the loss and bring justice to those responsible for the attack.

The plot is filled with tension, as Major Vihaan must not only deal with the psychological toll of war but also the complexities of loyalty, honor, and revenge. The operation itself is a true testament to the precision and planning of the Indian Army, providing a gripping, fast-paced narrative that will keep viewers on the edge of their seats.

Performances

Vicky Kaushal delivers a stellar performance as Major Vihaan Singh Shergill, capturing the emotional and physical toll of the mission while portraying the calm, methodical, and strong-willed character. His portrayal of a soldier caught between patriotism and personal loss is highly commendable.

Paresh Rawal plays National Security Advisor Govind Bharadwaj, who is responsible for planning the surgical strike. His character adds a layer of complexity to the narrative, reflecting the emotional burden of making tough decisions for the greater good.

The supporting cast, including Yami Gautam as the RAW officer and Mohit Raina as Major Vihaan’s colleague, also bring strong performances, adding depth and nuance to the film’s narrative.

Direction & Screenplay

Aditya Dhar's direction is sharp and precise, ensuring the film remains engaging throughout. The screenplay expertly balances action with emotion, allowing the audience to connect with the characters while maintaining an intense pace during the operation sequences. The film avoids unnecessary dramatization, sticking to the realism of the operation, which enhances its impact.

The film’s strong point is its focus on the personal and professional lives of the characters, bringing out their motivations, struggles, and sacrifices. It also highlights the unity and determination of the Indian Army, showing the human side of soldiers in the face of an intense mission.

Cinematography & Music

The cinematography in Uri is exceptional, especially in the action scenes. The film makes effective use of its settings, from the rugged terrains of the military camp to the tense, covert operation sequences. The visuals are sharp, and the cinematographic techniques amplify the intensity of the action sequences, particularly during the surgical strike itself.

The music, composed by Shashwat Sachdev, works well with the film’s tone, creating a sense of urgency and patriotism. The background score is particularly effective in intensifying the emotional beats, and the soundtrack complements the action scenes with patriotic fervor, making it a powerful element of the film’s overall appeal.

Pacing & Editing

The film has a runtime of around 138 minutes, which is well-paced considering the intensity of the material. The editing is tight and effective, keeping the story flowing smoothly from one action set-piece to another while allowing for moments of emotional reflection. The final act of the film, which deals with the aftermath of the operation, is particularly well-handled, giving the audience a sense of closure.

User's Personal Opinion

As someone who deeply respects the sacrifices made by the armed forces, Uri: The Surgical Strike hits home on a personal level. The film captures the resilience, strength, and determination of the soldiers in a way that is both motivating and humbling. The narrative isn’t just about the operation; it’s about the personal stories behind it—the soldiers who risk their lives and the emotional toll of carrying out such missions.

What stands out is the film’s ability to invoke patriotism without resorting to jingoism. It shows the human side of war and its impact on the lives of the soldiers, their families, and their comrades. It’s a reminder of the sacrifices made by those who protect the nation, and it makes you feel a deep sense of pride and respect for the Indian Armed Forces.

Final Thoughts

Uri: The Surgical Strike is an intense, gripping, and well-made war film that successfully captures the heroism, strategy, and emotional complexities of the 2016 surgical strike. With its strong performances, tight direction, and powerful message, it is a must-watch for fans of war dramas and those who appreciate films that honor the sacrifices made by our soldiers.

Rating: 4.5/5

Final Thoughts: Uri is a film that will stay with you, invoking feelings of pride, admiration, and respect for the men and women who serve the nation. It’s a true testament to their strength, courage, and unwavering commitment to protect the country.
