Rekhachithram - Movie Review

Movie Title: Rekhachithram

Genre: Mystery Thriller

Director: Jofin T. Chacko

Release Date: 2025

Cast: Asif Ali, Anaswara Rajan, Manoj K. Jayan, Siddique

Review

Rekhachithram, directed by Jofin T. Chacko, is a masterpiece that explores the eerie intersection of history, cinema, and crime through a complex narrative. The film blends investigative thriller tropes with a subtle but powerful homage to Malayalam cinema, making it a unique piece in the landscape of Indian cinema.

Plot & Storyline

At the heart of Rekhachithram lies the investigation of a suicide, which uncovers a tangled web of crime, loss, and betrayal. The plot weaves two timelines—one set in the present, following the investigation by CI Vivek Gopinath (played by Asif Ali), and the other rooted in the past, touching on events surrounding the disappearance of a young actress, Rekha, during the filming of *Kathodu Kathoram* in 1985.

The film reimagines an alternate history, blending fiction and reality by using the making of *Kathodu Kathoram* as a backdrop. This exploration of cinema’s influence on life is what sets the movie apart from typical murder mysteries, as it reflects on how the medium affects those involved, both onscreen and offscreen.

Performances

Asif Ali, playing the role of Vivek Gopinath, anchors the film with a deep emotional performance that adds gravitas to the film’s investigation. Anaswara Rajan delivers a poignant portrayal of Rekha, a young actress whose life is deeply intertwined with the film industry. Manoj K. Jayan and Siddique also stand out in their roles, contributing to the film’s compelling performances.

Direction & Screenplay

The direction by Jofin T. Chacko and screenplay by John Manthrikal and Ramu Sunil are tightly executed, especially in how they manage to switch between timelines seamlessly. The slow build-up of the investigation adds to the suspense, while allowing the emotional arcs of the characters to unfold naturally.

Cinematography & Music

The cinematography by Appu Prabhakar perfectly captures the eerie beauty of Malakkappara’s forests and the nostalgia of 1980s Kerala. The music by Mujeeb Majeed enhances the emotional depth, complementing the film’s dark tone.

Pacing & Editing

The film’s pacing is deliberate, with a slow unraveling of its mystery. Some may find it slow in the first half, but for those who appreciate a more patient and thoughtful thriller, this works to the film’s advantage.

User's Personal Opinion

What makes Rekhachithram truly stand out is its reflection on cinema's influence on reality. The film asks thought-provoking questions about how films shape the way we perceive the world around us. It cleverly integrates real-life references and explores the impact of a 1985 film on present-day lives, a fresh perspective not often explored in Indian cinema.

Final Thoughts

Rekhachithram is not just a thriller, but a film that honors the cultural impact of cinema. With its gripping screenplay, superb performances, and captivating direction, it is a must-watch for anyone who appreciates films that blend emotional depth with genre storytelling.

Rating: 4.5/5

Final Thoughts: Rekhachithram is a triumph, offering a unique cinematic experience that resonates with cinephiles and mystery lovers alike.
